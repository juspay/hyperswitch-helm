# Product Context

_This document explains why this project exists, the problems it solves, how it should work, and the user experience goals._

## Why Hyperswitch Exists

Hyperswitch aims to simplify and democratize payment processing for digital businesses. Traditional payment systems can be complex, expensive, and lock businesses into specific providers. Hyperswitch offers an open, flexible alternative.

## Problems It Solves

*   **Vendor Lock-in:** Reduces dependency on single payment processors.
*   **High Costs:** Aims to provide more affordable payment solutions.
*   **Integration Complexity:** Simplifies the process of connecting to multiple payment gateways.
*   **Lack of Control:** Gives businesses more control over their payment stack.
*   **Slow Innovation:** Being community-led, it can adapt and innovate faster.

## How It Should Work

*   **Abstraction Layer:** Acts as an abstraction layer over various payment processors, allowing businesses to switch or use multiple processors seamlessly.
*   **Unified API:** Provides a single API for interacting with different payment gateways.
*   **Centralized Management:** Offers a control center for managing configurations, viewing analytics, and testing payments.
*   **Secure Data Handling:** Includes a card vault for securely managing sensitive payment information.
*   **Scalable Architecture:** Designed to handle a high volume of transactions reliably.

## User Experience Goals

*   **Ease of Integration:** Developers should find it straightforward to integrate Hyperswitch into their applications.
*   **Simplified Management:** The control center should be intuitive and easy to use for managing payment operations.
*   **Reliability and Performance:** Users should experience fast and reliable payment processing.
*   **Transparency:** Provide clear insights into payment flows and costs.
*   **Developer-Friendly:** Offer good documentation, SDKs, and tools for developers.
